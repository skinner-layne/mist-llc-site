Security & Compliance

Security and compliance.

Mist Dynamics is the named legal entity on every contract. Your DPA, MSA, and audit reports name Mist Dynamics LLC, not a brand. Procurement teams get a single counterparty.

Data handling

Where your data lives, who touches it, how long we keep it.

Encryption

All traffic to Mist is TLS 1.3. Data at rest is AES-256 encrypted. Secrets — including provider API keys — are stored in an HSM-backed vault and never written to disk in plaintext.

Training opt-out

Prompts and responses routed through Mist are not used for model training by Mist Dynamics. Where Mist routes to a third-party provider, we use that provider’s zero-retention enterprise endpoint when one is available, and we contractually prohibit training on customer data on every relationship where that’s an option.

Retention

Prompts and responses are not retained beyond billing reconciliation — typically 7 days. Hashes, token counts, latencies, and routing decisions are retained per your configured audit log retention (90 days on Team, 1 year on Business, configurable on Enterprise).

Data residency

Enterprise customers can pin all routing to US, EU, or APAC regions. Mist will exclude any provider that cannot serve a request from the required region. Audit logs are stored in the same region as the traffic.

Identity & Access

Single sign-on, role-based access, just-in-time deprovisioning.

SSO

SAML 2.0 and OIDC supported on every plan, not gated to Enterprise. Tested with Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, OneLogin, JumpCloud.

SCIM

SCIM 2.0 user provisioning. When you offboard an employee in your IdP, their Mist access ends within minutes.

RBAC

Five built-in roles: Admin, Billing, Engineer, Auditor, Viewer. Custom roles on Enterprise.

API key scoping

Scope each API key to a project, a model set, a spend cap, and a list of allowed source IPs.

Audit logs

Every admin action, every key creation, every config change — logged with actor, time, and prior-state diff.

MFA

WebAuthn (passkeys) and TOTP supported. MFA enforcement configurable per role.

Provider relationships

Direct enterprise contracts. Not redistributed retail.

Mist Dynamics holds direct enterprise accounts with each provider in our routing fabric. Your traffic flows through commercially-papered relationships, not through someone else’s consumer API key.

This matters for three reasons:

  • Liability. If something goes wrong upstream, Mist Dynamics is the counterparty in the chain — not a Reddit post about a hobbyist proxy.
  • Rate limits. Enterprise accounts get enterprise rate limits. Your traffic isn’t fighting hobbyist quotas during a launch.
  • Data terms. Enterprise endpoints typically include zero-retention and no-training defaults. Retail endpoints often don’t.
Infrastructure

Multi-region. Automated failover. HSM-backed secrets.

Multi-region deployment

Mist runs in three independent regions (US-East, US-West, EU-Central) with active-active load balancing. Regional failover is automatic.

Secrets management

Provider API keys live in an HSM-backed secrets vault with hardware-rooted attestation. Key rotation is automated and logged.

Network isolation

Routing infrastructure runs in a VPC with no public ingress except the API gateway. Internal traffic is mTLS-authenticated.

Vulnerability management

Continuous dependency scanning, weekly patch cadence, quarterly third-party penetration tests. Critical vulnerabilities patched within 24 hours.

Incident response

When something goes wrong, we tell you.

We publish a status page, run a 24/7 on-call rotation, and commit to root-cause analysis on every incident that affects customer traffic.

  • Status page. status.mist.llc — updated within 5 minutes of incident detection.
  • On-call rotation. 24/7 engineering on-call with a 15-minute response SLA on production incidents.
  • Customer notification. Email and webhook alerts for any incident affecting your traffic. Enterprise customers also get a phone tree.
  • Post-incident RCA. Every customer-affecting incident gets a written root-cause analysis published within five business days. No exceptions.
  • Breach notification. Notification within 72 hours of confirmed breach, per GDPR Article 33 standards, regardless of whether GDPR applies to your jurisdiction.
