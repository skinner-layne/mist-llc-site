Privacy Policy

Effective April 7, 2026

Mist Dynamics LLC (“Mist,” “we,” “us”) is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and disclose information in connection with the Mist API and related services (the “Services”).

1. Information We Collect

We collect information in the following categories:

  • Account information. Name, email address, company name, billing address, and payment details, provided when creating an account or signing a contract.
  • Authentication data. Login credentials, SSO assertions, MFA tokens.
  • API usage data. API keys, request metadata (timestamps, model selected, token counts, latency, source IP), and routing decisions. This data is logged for billing, audit, and operational purposes.
  • Prompts and responses. The content of API requests and responses passing through the Services. See Section 4 for retention details.
  • Support communications. Information you provide when contacting support, sales, or security.
  • Cookies and analytics. Standard web analytics on our marketing website. We do not place tracking cookies on the API endpoint.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Services.
  • Route API requests to appropriate Provider endpoints.
  • Bill for usage and reconcile invoices.
  • Enforce acceptable use policies and detect abuse.
  • Respond to support, sales, security, and legal inquiries.
  • Comply with legal obligations.

3. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal information. We share information only as necessary to provide the Services, and only with the following categories of recipients:

  • Model Providers. We forward prompts and responses to the Provider selected by the routing logic in order to fulfill the request. Each Provider operates under its own contract with Mist that includes data protection terms.
  • Subprocessors. Cloud infrastructure, payment processing, and identity verification vendors. A current list is available in our Data Processing Addendum.
  • Legal authorities. Where required by valid legal process. We will notify the affected customer unless legally prohibited from doing so.
  • In a corporate transaction. If Mist is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, customer information may transfer as part of the transaction, subject to the same protections.

We do not use prompts or responses for model training, and we contractually prohibit our Providers from doing so where that option is available.

4. Data Retention

Different categories of data have different retention periods:

  • Prompts and responses: Retained for billing reconciliation only, typically not more than 7 days, and then deleted.
  • API request metadata (audit logs): Retained per the customer’s plan — 90 days on Team, 1 year on Business, configurable on Enterprise.
  • Account and billing information: Retained for the duration of the customer relationship and for as long as required by tax and accounting law thereafter.
  • Support communications: Retained for two years after the conversation ends.

5. GDPR Rights

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • The right to access your personal information.
  • The right to correct inaccurate information.
  • The right to delete personal information, subject to legal exceptions.
  • The right to restrict or object to certain processing.
  • The right to data portability.
  • The right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

To exercise these rights, contact privacy@mist.llc.

6. CCPA Rights

If you are a California resident, you have the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete personal information, the right to correct inaccurate information, the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell personal information), and the right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights. Contact privacy@mist.llc to exercise any of these rights.

7. International Transfers

Mist operates in the United States and the European Union. When personal information is transferred from the EU/EEA, UK, or Switzerland to the United States, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission to ensure adequate protection.

8. Children’s Privacy

The Services are not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If you become aware that a child has provided us personal information, contact privacy@mist.llc and we will take appropriate action.

9. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information. See our Security page for additional detail. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we work to protect your information using commercially reasonable means.

10. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated by email to account contacts at least 30 days before they take effect.

11. Contact

Questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices:
privacy@mist.llc
